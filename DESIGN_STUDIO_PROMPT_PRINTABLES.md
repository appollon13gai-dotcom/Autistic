# Compás TEA AI — Design Specification for Printable Materials Only

## Project summary (focused on printables)
This is a standalone prompt for designing the printable materials for "Compás TEA AI" — a daily skill-training gym for children aged 5 with autism (ASD/TEA suspicion). 

The printables are a core, child-facing part of the app: schedule cards, visual exercise instructions, reward trackers, maps, flag collections, and motivational sheets. They must be highly engaging, independent-use friendly for the child, and fully integrated with the "Спортзал Суперзверей" (Supercreature Gym) concept.

Primary users of printables: the 5-year-old child (with parent support). Goal: make the child *want* to use them, look at them, collect them, and feel proud — turning therapy-like activities into exciting, repeatable play.

The app supplements professional therapy (Atención Temprana etc.), it does not replace it. Printables are used at home alongside the digital app.

---

## Core concept for printables: "Спортзал Суперзверей" (Supercreature Gym) — Printable Edition

The child is the HERO and COACH. The companion character is the child's teammate in the gym. Printables make the child feel like they are collecting real "adventures" and "badges" from their favorite theme (world flags & geography).

### Character & Theme Integration (FLAGS/GEOGRAPHY as PRIMARY SPECIAL INTEREST)
- Companion character (dinosaur / fox / bear / cat — chosen by child) is a "World Explorer" who collects flags and visits countries.
- Child's name + character name on every printable (e.g., "Натан и Рэкс исследуют мир").
- EVERY printable is themed around flags and geography:
  - Flag icons, simplified country maps/outlines, compass roses, passport stamps, "travel" borders.
  - Exercise visuals show the character "traveling" to a country, holding the flag, standing on the map.
  - Reward trackers: slots for collecting flag "stickers" or coloring in countries on a world map.
  - Schedule cards: themed as "Today's Expedition" with flag borders and map elements.
- Special interest personalization: All visuals, icons, and language adapt to flags/geography (e.g., "Collect the flag of France!", "Rэкс found the Eiffel Tower!").
- Child can color, sticker, or draw on printables — make them interactive and forgiving (thick lines, large areas).

### Naming & Language (example: interest = world flags & geography, child = Natan, character = Рэкс the dino explorer)
- Currency/rewards: "флажки" (flags) or "паспортные штампы" (passport stamps).
- Titles: "Экспедиция: Флаг Франции", "Карта мира Рэкса", "Собери флаги континентов".
- Character speech on printables: "Рэкс говорит: 'Смотри, этот флаг из страны с пирамидами!'"
- Progress: "Натан и Рэкс посетили 12 стран! Коллекция флагов: 47/195"

## Research & Evidence Base (must inform printable design)
Ground all designs in these:

- **Visual Supports (TEACCH, NCAEP 28 Evidence-Based Practices, Odom et al. research)**: Visual schedules, choice boards, picture sequences, and icons paired with minimal text dramatically improve independence, task completion, and reduced anxiety in autistic preschoolers. Use clear, high-contrast icons; consistent layouts across all printables; step-by-step picture instructions.

- **Otsimo (autism apps research and design)**: Highly visual, icon-heavy interfaces with PECS-style communication and gamified elements increase engagement and voluntary use. Apply to printables: large flag/country icons, minimal text, predictable grid layouts, collecting mechanics.

- **Hopebridge & similar ABA/play-based programs**: Use visual token economies, immediate success visuals, and themed rewards. Printables should have clear "collect here" reward trackers and celebration elements (e.g., "You earned this flag!").

- **Gamification & Special Interests (game-based learning research for ASD, ESDM principles)**: Collecting items (flags), levels/progress maps, and tying everything to the child's intense interest (flags/geography) significantly boost attention, motivation, and desire to repeat. Children with ASD often sustain engagement 2-5x longer with interest-based visuals.

- **Sensory & Design Research (autistic UX studies, color research for ASD, TEACCH sensory guidelines)**: Autistic children prefer subtle blue/green hues, high contrast without brightness overload, rounded/simple shapes, and low-clutter designs. Use muted joyful palettes per theme, large clear elements, white space. Avoid dense text or chaotic layouts.

- **Predictability & Structure (TEACCH)**: Consistent header/footer, numbered steps with pictures, "what you need" visual list, and "done" checkbox on every printable reduce anxiety and support independent use.

- **Special Interests as Attention Trigger (multiple ASD intervention studies)**: For this child, flags and world map locations are the #1 hook. Every printable must scream "flags + geography adventure". Use real (simplified, recognizable) flags, country shapes, and basic map elements. This turns printables into desirable "collectibles" the child will ask for.

## Attention Triggers & Engagement for Printables (Expanded, with Flags Focus)
Research shows these triggers work especially well for printables with autistic preschoolers. Every design element must activate them, with heavy emphasis on flags/geography:

- **Special Interests (Highest Impact)**: Flags, maps, countries, "traveling the world". Child will stare at, point to, color, and request more flag-themed pages. Make the entire printable an "expedition log" or "flag album page". Examples: border of flags, map with pins for collected countries, "passport" style with stamp spaces.

- **Visual Supports & Icons**: Large, clear flag icons (high contrast, simple), step-by-step picture sequences (child doing the exercise with the character), visual choice boards or "what you need" lists with pictures. Pair minimal text with icons.

- **Gamification & Collecting**: Reward trackers with 5 flag slots (color in or sticker the flag when done). World map progress (color countries as you "visit"). "Collect the set" feeling — e.g., "Africa flags" page. Badges/medals as flag stickers.

- **Predictability & Clear Structure**: Same layout on every card (header with character + title, steps with pictures, reward area at bottom, checkbox). Numbered steps, "start here → end here" arrows.

- **Immediate Success & Celebration**: Big "done" checkbox that looks satisfying when filled. Space for child to add their own sticker or drawing. Character saying "Great job collecting this flag!"

- **Sensory-Friendly**: Calm base colors with flag pops of color. Large white space. Thick lines for coloring. No small details that frustrate.

- **Mastery & Pride**: Visible collection (growing flag album feel). "You are a world explorer!" messaging. Photo/sticker placeholder so child can personalize ("My flag drawing here").

- **Multi-Modal/Interactive Print**: Thick enough for stickers, coloring, or laminating. Space for child to draw the flag or country.

Incorporate flags/geography everywhere: character "travels" by collecting flags, progress is a world map filling up, exercises involve "finding" flags on maps, reward is adding a real flag sticker.

## Design Requirements for Printables
- **Format**: A4 / Letter size, print-ready (high resolution, CMYK-friendly if possible, but RGB OK for home printing). Margins safe for home printers (at least 0.5 inch / 1.27 cm).
- **Style**: Match the digital app — rounded shapes (18-22px equivalent), soft shadows if printable, Nunito or similar rounded sans-serif font. High contrast, large text (titles 24pt+, body 14pt+).
- **Child-Friendly**:
  - Maximum visuals, minimum text.
  - Large checkboxes and coloring areas.
  - Thick, simple lines.
  - Engaging but calm illustrations of the companion character interacting with flags/maps.
  - Space for child agency (coloring, stickers, drawing).
- **Gamification Elements**: Reward trackers (5-10 slots per page), progress maps (world map with countries to color/flag), "passport" stamps, collection grids ("Collect all Europe flags").
- **Structure per Printable**:
  - Header: Companion emoji/illustration + child's name + title in special interest language + date line.
  - Visual list of 4-8 exercises (icon + short label + checkbox).
  - Bottom reward tracker + "You are a Super Explorer!" message.
  - Photo/sticker placeholder.
  - Small character speech bubble with flag fact or encouragement.
- **Sets to Design**:
  - Daily/Weekly Schedule Cards (themed as "Today's Expedition").
  - Individual Exercise Visual Instructions (step-by-step with pictures, for printing and laminating).
  - Reward/Collection Pages (flag album, world map progress, badge sheets).
  - Themed packs (e.g., "Europe Flags Expedition", "Continents Adventure").
- **Language Support**: RU (default), ES, EN, UA. Text must be large, clear, and easy to translate. Design so language switch doesn't break layout.
- **Accessibility**: High contrast versions available. Designs work in black & white too (for simple home printing).

## Attention Triggers Specific to Printables (with Flags/Geography)
- **Collecting & Ownership**: Child "owns" the flags they collect. Physical stickers or coloring the map gives tactile reward.
- **Visual Mastery**: Seeing a growing collection of colorful flags on their wall or in a binder is highly motivating.
- **Geography Adventure Narrative**: "We traveled to France today!" makes abstract skills concrete and exciting.
- **Immediate Checkbox Win**: Big, satisfying checkbox next to each flag/exercise.
- **Personalization**: Space for child's drawing or photo with the flag.
- **Repetition with Variation**: Same structure every time, but new flags/countries keep it fresh.
- **Pride Display**: Designed to be hung up or put in a "Explorer's Book".

---

## What NOT to do for Printables
- No dense text blocks.
- No tiny flags or details that are hard to color/see.
- No clinical language ("exercise", "skill", "therapy").
- No failure states or "try again" in a negative way.
- No overwhelming colors or patterns.
- Avoid generic clipart — use consistent, themed character + flag style.
- Do not make them look like worksheets from school/therapy.

Use the child's flags/geography interest as the #1 design driver.
